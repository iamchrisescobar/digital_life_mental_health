# Annotated Bibliography (Alphabetized)

---

## Abi-Jaoude, Elia et al. (2020)  
**“Smartphones, social media use and youth mental health.”**  
*CMAJ*, Vol. 192, No. 6, pp. E136–E141.  
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1503/cmaj.190434  
https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7012622/

The article argues that smartphone and social media use correlate with the increase in mental distress, self-injurious behavior, and suicidality among youth. The evidence comes from multiple previous research studies and reviews. This source is important because it explores different sub-parts of mental health such as self-harm, sleep loss, and social skills.

---

## Berryman, Chloe, Ferguson, Christopher J., & Negy, Charles (2018)  
**“Social media use and mental health among young adults.”**  
*Psychiatric Quarterly*, Vol. 89, No. 2  
https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11126-017-9535-6

This paper highlights how specific online behaviors such as vague booking are associated with loneliness and suicidal thoughts. It challenges the assumption that all social media use is harmful and emphasizes that quality of interaction matters more than quantity.

---

## Braghieri, Luca, Levy, Ro’ee, & Makarin, Alexey (2022)  
**“Social Media and Mental Health.”**  
*American Economic Review*, Vol. 112, No. 11  
https://www.aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/aer.20211218

This article provides strong causal evidence that Facebook adoption increased depression and anxiety among college students using large-scale economic datasets.

---

## Coyne, Sarah M. et al. (2020)  
**“Does time spent using social media impact mental health?”**  
*Computers in Human Behavior*, Vol. 104  
https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0747563219303723

This eight-year longitudinal study found that increased time spent on social media is not causally associated with long-term anxiety or depression.

---

## Craig, Shelley L. et al. (2021)  
**“Can Social Media Participation Enhance LGBTQ+ Youth Well-Being?”**  
*Social Media + Society*, Vol. 7, No. 1  
https://doi.org/10.1177/2056305121988931

This study introduced the Social Media Benefits Scale and identified positive mental health benefits for LGBTQ+ youth.

---

## Feijoo, Beatriz & Vizcaino-Verdu, Arantxa (2025)  
**“The Impact of Social Media Aesthetic Capital on Adolescent Body Image.”**  
https://doi.org/10.1037/ppm0000637

This study shows how influencer culture reinforces anxiety, self-comparison, and body dissatisfaction.

---

## Garg, Muskan (2023)  
**“Mental Health Analysis in Social Media Posts: A Survey.”**  
*Archives of Computational Methods in Engineering*, Vol. 30  
https://doi.org/10.1007/s11831-022-09863-z

This article explains how machine learning can detect depression and emotional distress using social media.

---

## Harmony Healthcare IT / Kaylin (2025)  
**Phone Screen Time Addiction & Usage – New Survey Data & Statistics**  
https://www.harmonyhit.com/phone-screen-time-statistics/

This report provides recent national statistics on screen addiction and perceived mental health impact.

---

## Hong, Wei et al. (2021)  
**“Social Media Exposure and Mental Health during COVID-19.”**  
https://doi.org/10.1089/cyber.2020.0387

This study highlights rumination and mindfulness as key psychological mediators of stress.

---

## Knowles, Ellen A. & Danzi, BreAnne A. (2025)  
**“The Role of Online Social Support in Mental Health.”**  
*Children*, Vol. 12  
https://doi.org/10.3390/children12020113

This research shows stronger mental health benefits of social media among rural youth.

---

## Lavis, Anna & Winter, Rachel (2020)  
**“Online Harms or Benefits?”**  
*Journal of Child Psychology and Psychiatry*, Vol. 61  
https://doi.org/10.1111/jcpp.13245

This ethnographic study shows peer support for self-harm can be protective.

---

## Naslund, John A. et al. (2020)  
**“Social media and mental health: benefits, risks, and opportunities.”**  
*Journal of Technology in Behavioral Science*, Vol. 5

This article discusses both positive and negative effects of social media on mental health.

---

## Nesi, Jacqueline (2020)  
**“The Impact of Social Media on Youth Mental Health: Challenges and Opportunities.”**  
*North Carolina Medical Journal*, Vol. 81  
https://ncmedicaljournal.com/article/55247

This commentary explores both risks and benefits of social media use.

---

## Niu, Yanzhuo et al. (2024)  
**“Shelter in the Online World?”**  
*Translational Issues in Psychological Science*, Vol. 10  
https://doi.org/10.1037/tps0000414

This study found both harmful and protective effects of social media during COVID-19.

---

## Panchal, Nirmita (2024)  
**“Racial and Ethnic Disparities in Mental Health Care.”**  
KFF  
https://www.kff.org/racial-equity-and-health-policy/

This report documents racial disparities in mental healthcare access.

---

## Roberts, James et al. (2014)  
**“The Invisible Addiction: Cell-Phone Activities and Addiction.”**  
*Journal of Behavioral Addictions*, Vol. 3  
https://doi.org/10.1556/JBA.3.2014.015

This study identifies platform-based addictive behaviors among college students.

---

## Scheinbaum, Angeline Close (Ed.). (2024)  
**The Darker Side of Social Media: Consumer Psychology and Mental Health**  
Routledge

This book explores psychological manipulation and self-esteem damage from social media.

---

## Shannon, Holly et al. (2022)  
**“Problematic Social Media Use in Adolescents.”**  
*JMIR Mental Health*  
https://doi.org/10.2196/33450

This meta-analysis confirms correlation but not causation between social media and poor mental health.

---

## Twenge, Jean M. et al. (2018)  
**“Decreases in Psychological Well-Being among American Adolescents after 2012.”**  
*Emotion*, Vol. 18  
https://doi.org/10.1037/emo0000403

This study links the rise of smartphones to declining adolescent well-being.

---

## UC Davis Health (2024)  
**“Social Media’s Impact on Our Mental Health and Tips to Use It Safely.”**  
https://health.ucdavis.edu/blog/cultivating-health/social-medias-impact-our-mental-health-and-tips-to-use-it-safely

This blog explains dopamine, FOMO, and cyberbullying as drivers of anxiety and depression.

---

## Valkenburg, Patti M. et al. (2022)  
**“Social media use and its impact on adolescent mental health.”**  
*Current Opinion in Psychology*, Vol. 44  
https://doi.org/10.1016/j.copsyc.2021.08.017

This umbrella review finds mostly weak or inconsistent associations between social media and mental health.

---

## Vornholt, Piper & De Choudhury, Munmun (2021)  
**“Understanding Social Media–Based Mental Health Support.”**  
*JMIR Mental Health*, Vol. 8  
https://doi.org/10.2196/24512

This study shows reliance on social media for mental health support due to limited therapy access.
